How It Works

Five simple steps. Fits your family's day.

SoundSteps turns speech practice into gentle, structured moments — no expertise required, just your presence and encouragement.

Listen to a Sound

Hear clear, targeted speech sounds in a calm, distraction-free environment — showing how each sound is correctly formed.

Audio-guided

Child Repeats the Sound

Your child is given a gentle, unpressured moment to try repeating the sound — building confidence gradually at their own pace.

Child-paced

Parent Encourages

You lead the session. SoundSteps guides you with prompts on how to offer positive reinforcement and gentle corrections without frustration.

Parent-guided

Recordings Are Saved

Each session is securely logged — building a timeline of progress that you can review and eventually share with professionals.

Secure & private

Gentle Progress Suggestions

The app reflects on patterns across sessions and offers kind, actionable suggestions to help guide your next practice.

Personalised
Why We Built This

SoundSteps began from our own family's experience.

Built by parents who understand

  • Long waiting lists with no clear guidance
  • Appointments cancelled or rearranged
  • Wanting to help at home, but unsure how
  • A need for something calm, practical, and accessible

"We didn't set out to build an app. We set out to help our children — and realised thousands of other families needed the same thing."

SoundSteps began from our own family's experience.

Our eldest child struggled with speech development. When he started nursery many people struggled to understand him. Over time his closest friends learned to understand what he was saying, sometimes even helping teachers interpret his speech at school.

Although he received some early support, speech development has remained something we continue to work on even now.

Our younger child is now facing similar challenges, but accessing support has become even more difficult. Waiting lists are longer, appointments are often cancelled or rearranged, and families can spend long periods unsure how best to help their child practise at home.

This experience led us to start building SoundSteps — a simple tool designed to help families practise speech sounds regularly at home while waiting for or alongside professional support.
